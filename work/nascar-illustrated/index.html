NASCAR Illustrated

A leading sports publication goes mobile.

  • ← See all work

4 weeks before the 2014 season began, the folks at NASCAR Illustrated came in with a request to update their site. They’ve been looking to move away from their old, years-untouched site into something more befitting. The jokes about speed and generally moving fast will write themselves, so I won’t be making any here.

It was important to get it right, and do it quickly. I provided some quick (and intentionally super undesigned) wireframes to make sure we’ve placed the proper emphasis on what’s important to them: selling subscriptions by promoting their latest issue.

Providing real HTML helped us have better conversations on whether or not we were heading in the right direction. It was important to see the thing we were building, rather than discuss a picture of it by looking at a mockup.

Once we had the hierarchy and flow down, I provided style tiles with a few visual directions and key elements. We iterated on the one that we liked and began to apply those styles to the default Bootstrap styling. Say what you will about Bootstrap, but it helped us get to the right spot quickly—we also tweaked it to run super fast when we put it into production.

We hooked this up to their existing CMS to let them manage and fulfill orders, as well as add new products to the site.

The first weekend it went live, sales shot through the roof—they did several times their normal volume. Lots of those transactions came from mobile devices, too.

What I learned

  • Making a mobile-friendly site helps boost your conversions.
  • If you have to move super fast, Bootstrap is a godsend.
  • Showing real HTML and style tiles helps you communicate ideas with more clarity. Especially to folks who aren’t designers.
