Twalkin

Twitter, but with a voice.

  • ← See all work

In the mid-to-late 00’s, social media was The Big Thing. "OMG Twitter! Look at this amazing breakfast, you guys,” we’d say. And then we’d talk about it with our friends. I loved the idea, despite never being able to share my food pictures with anyone because I was too busy eating them.

I don’t usually show work this old (5 years is ancient in the digital space), but I was pleased to have helped solve a number of technical challenges in doing this. We used IVR technology to help make these calls possible. These IVRs were configured to let us manage these calls via a web or phone interface, too.

There was a startup who wanted to make a voice-based social network that integrated with Twitter. We all had these amazing phones and the App Store was still brand new. Why not take as much advantage of the phone aspect as we could? I came on to lead the creative, front-end and branding efforts.

We designed this for a few types of people: celebrities, businesses, and friends. Celebrities were looking for new ways to talk with their fans, and Twalkin allowed them to have a platform to do so. Businesses who wanted to use it could hold press briefings and let anyone who wanted join the call (or it could be restricted by password if it was something secret). Friends would also call in and talk with everyone without the hassle of 3-way calling all their friends to get on the line.

Since this could easily get out of hand with several-to-a-few-hundred people on the line, we built some heavy-duty management features that felt light to the touch. One could start out with their call muted and un-mute anyone they wanted (or boot them if they misbehaved). We provided a smart, consistent visual interface that worked on the app and the website. Both had the same features and functionality—no shortchanging any mobile users here.
