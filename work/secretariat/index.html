Secretariat

Not now, I’m working.

  • ← See all work

As a designer who uses his phone for testing, I wanted to be able to use my device without being bothered by emails or phone calls. I’ve already turned off notifications, but phone calls would still come through. I needed to be able to selectively turn those things off and focus.

I started on an idea that was ultimately called Secretariat. It would let you have quiet times by turning off your phone and email delivery any time you wanted and could automatically reply to would-be interrupters that you’ll be back soon.

Taking care to let important things through was, well, important. If someone emailed with Emergency in the subject line of an email, you could get an alert that let you know something was going down that required your attention.

I also wanted to explore early 1900’s design, so I did several explorations into the bombastic imagery and typography of that era to help me come up with the aesthetic you see here. That was a ton of fun.

If this sounds vaguely similar to Do Not Disturb on your iPhone, you’re right—I started this before iOS adopted it. It was close enough to my idea that it didn’t make sense to continue the project.
