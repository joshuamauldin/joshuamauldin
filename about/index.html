A brief introduction

I’m interested in approaching things with as much humanity and curiosity as I can muster. I believe that design should be invisible, have a voice, and be delightful to use. Hold the BS, please and thank you.

I live in the borderlands of design and development by writing copy, hammering out code, getting into command line tools, and designing apps. I also constantly photograph things and occasionally make album art. This generalist way of thinking gives me a big toolbox that I’m always trying to grow because it helps me be the best I can.

I’m responsible for what I put into the world, so seeing to the long-term health of a product is more exciting to me than a one-off project. Those can be fun, but it feels more rewarding to me if I perfect something over time.

Currently living in Charlotte, NC by way of Los Angeles, by way of Seattle…by way of Charlotte.

The Reverse Resume

Instead of a standard "I’ve worked here, I know this" thing (I try to know a little of everything), I’d rather talk about the types of projects and people that interest me. Inspired by Tony Wright.

The team

  • We care about the product and the people who use it.
  • We drive each other to be better.
  • We come up with the best product by using data, testing, and quick iterations.
  • We’re empathetic, ever-hopeful, honest, and like each other.
  • We are good citizens, not mercenaries.

The work

  • Design and problem solving are core values.
  • We work seamlessly with other departments to provide insights on improving the product.
  • We focus on what’s best for people without shortchanging them to sate a short-term goal.
  • Politics: be damned. You too, egos.
  • Questions, exploring new possibilities, and a healthy questioning of “business as usual” are encouraged.
